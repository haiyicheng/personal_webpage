## The Moderating Role of Early Life Family Environment on the Education-Health Relationship

**Presented at:** Population Association of America 2025 Annual Meeting; American Sociological Association 2025 Annual Meeting

**Authors:** Haiyi Cheng, Wendy Yiqing Chao

### Overview

This project investigates the complex relationship between education and health, focusing on how early life family environments shape the returns to education in terms of health outcomes. Drawing on life course perspectives and fundamental cause theory, we examine whether childhood family conditions moderate the protective effects of education on adult health.

### Methods

- Leveraged longitudinal data to trace how early-life family environments shape later health outcomes
- Examined moderation effects of childhood family conditions on the education-health gradient
- Employed life course analytical frameworks to capture temporal dynamics

### Key Contributions

- Advanced understanding of heterogeneity in the education-health relationship
- Highlighted the role of early-life conditions as moderators of socioeconomic gradients in health
- Presented findings at two major national conferences (PAA 2025 and ASA 2025)

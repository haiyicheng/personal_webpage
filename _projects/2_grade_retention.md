## Gender Disparities in Grade Retention among Asian Americans: Family Structure and Racial/Ethnic Variation

**Presented at:** American Sociological Association 2025 Annual Meeting

**Authors:** Haiyi Cheng

**Supervisor:** Dr. Fumiya Uchikoshi, The Harvard Academy for International and Area Studies, Harvard University

### Overview

This project examines how gender, race/ethnicity, and family structure interact to shape grade retention outcomes among Asian American students. Using large-scale survey data, I investigate whether the widely observed male disadvantage in grade retention varies across Asian ethnic subgroups and family structures.

### Methods

- Processed **ACS microdata (2008–2023)** from IPUMS
- Implemented **sibling fixed effects models** to examine three-way interactions between gender, race/ethnicity, and family structure
- Applied **linear probability models** with household-clustered standard errors to analyze grade retention patterns across Asian ethnic subgroups

### Key Contributions

- Disaggregated the "Asian American" category to reveal heterogeneity in educational outcomes
- Identified how family structure moderates gender disparities in grade retention
- Contributed to understanding the intersection of immigration, family, and education

## Does Pressured Internet Use Promote Chinese Older Adults' Mental Health?

**Status:** Revise and Resubmit at _Depression and Anxiety_

**Authors:** Long Li, Haiyi Cheng, Guangzhao Jin, Rize Jing

**Affiliation:** Center for Population and Development Studies, Renmin University of China

### Overview

The rapid digitalization accelerated by the COVID-19 pandemic has pushed many older adults into using Internet-based applications for essential services. This project distinguishes between **pressured Internet use** (driven by pandemic-era applications such as health codes and digital payments) and **voluntary Internet use** to examine their differential associations with mental health among Chinese older adults.

### Methods

- Applied **confirmatory latent class analysis** to distinguish pressured Internet use from voluntary Internet use patterns
- Employed **structural equation modelling (SEM)** to examine differential associations between Internet use types and mental health
- Investigated the **mediating role of subjective age** in the Internet use–mental health relationship

### Key Findings

- Pressured and voluntary Internet use have distinct pathways to mental health outcomes
- Subjective age mediates the relationship between Internet use types and mental well-being
- Policy implications for digital inclusion programs for older adults

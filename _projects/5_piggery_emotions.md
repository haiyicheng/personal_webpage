## "We Eat Our Pigs": How Economic Principles and Institutions Shape the Emotions of Piggery Employees

**Presented at:** American Sociological Association 2023 Annual Meeting; BSA Work, Employment & Society Conference 2023; East Asian Anthropological Association 2023 Annual Meeting

**Authors:** Haiyi Cheng

### Overview

This project explores how economic rationality and institutional structures shape the emotional lives of workers in China's pork industry. Through ethnographic fieldwork and in-depth interviews, I examine how piggery employees navigate the tension between affective bonds with animals and the economic imperatives of industrial livestock production.

### Methods

- Conducted extensive **ethnographic fieldwork** in pork production facilities
- Performed **semi-structured interviews** with piggery employees
- Applied **grounded theory** and **discourse analysis** to analyze emotional labor in agricultural settings

### Key Contributions

- Contributed to the sociology of emotions, work, and economic life
- Demonstrated how institutional arrangements mediate the relationship between economic rationality and emotional experience
- Received travel funding from ASA, BSA, and EAAA to present findings at three international conferences
